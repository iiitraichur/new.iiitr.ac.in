Percentage To GPA Conversion Rule

As for the percentage to GPA conversion, the following rule applies

  1. If a candidate's university has provided GPA corresponding to the percentage on the transcript, or if his/her university prescribes some formula for conversion, then that will apply. This formula should be mentioned clearly on the University website and/or candidate’s transcript.
  2. Otherwise, the following formula, will apply: GPA = percentage/10 If the GPA of the candidate is not in the scale of 10 but in the scale of K (e.g., 4 or 8), then: GPA = GPAOnScaleK*10/(K) Here, GPAOnScaleK is the candidate's GPA on the scale of K. For GPA, rounding-off will not be done. For example, if a candidate’s GPA is 6.98 and the GPA criterion is 7.00, then, the candidate will not be considered as eligible.


Financial assistance

Full time PhD students are eligible to receive financial assistance through various avenues.

  1. MOE Fellowships: As per current norms, MOE fellowships provide monthly stipend of ₹38750 for the first two years (at JRF level) and ₹43750 for the next three years (at SRF level).
  2. PM Fellowships: PM fellowships are very prestigious fellowships which pays double the amount as compared to MOE fellowship. These fellowships are quite competitive and require an industry partner, where the student will be working on research problems that are of mutual interest to a faculty member and the industry partner. Please see https://primeministerfellowshipscheme.in/ for more details.
  3. Visvesvaraya Fellowships: PhD students of CSE department can also apply for Visvesvaraya Fellowship which pays ₹31500 per month for the first two years of the program and ₹35000 per month. Additional HRA is provided, if applicable. It also has provision for some contingency funds. Please see http://phd.medialabasia.in/fulltimephd.php for more details about Visvesvaraya Fellowship.
  4. Industry fellowships: Companies such as Intel, Infosys, TCS, Google often provide fellowships for full time PhD students. Getting such fellowships also adds a plus point in your CV. These fellowship amounts vary but usually provides similar monthly stipends as MHRD fellowships.
  5. The institute provides financial assistance to present their research papers in international and national venues during their PhD program. To know more about the department and research interests of the faculty, please visit https://iiitr.ac.in/faculty.html

The department invites applications under both full-time and part-time categories in this admission round.

Eligibility Criteria for Mathematics

Candidates applying for the Ph.D. program need to possess the following qualifications

1.         M.A. / M.Sc. or Equivalent Degree in the concerned or respective department with minimum CPI of 6.0 out of 10 or minimum 60% of marks. Relaxation for SC/ST/PwD Candidates: The CPI or percentage marks in mentioned eligibility requirements will be relaxed by 0.5 CPI or by 5% marks for SC/ST/Pwd candidates.  

2.         The applicant must have already earned the Master’s degree, or should at least be in the final year of the respective program and it would be assumed that the candidate will be able to complete the degree by the time of admission into the Ph.D. program.

3.         For receiving assistantship in PhD programmes in Mathematics, a candidate must have a minimum qualifying Post Graduate Degree in Mathematics, selected through a process described through any one of the following:

a.         Scholars who are selected through National Eligibility Tests – UGC NET including lectureship (Assistant Professorship) and GATE

b.         The selection process through National level examinations conducted by MOE and its Agencies and Institutions such as UGC/IIT/IISc./IISER/IIIT, etc.
